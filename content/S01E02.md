# Urban Flooding and Digital Models: A Conversation with Franzi

From civil engineering student to urban flood researcher, Franzi Tügel demonstrates how water moves through cities in unexpected ways. Her fascination with water-related topics during her studies led her to focus on fluvial flooding—when heavy rainfall overwhelms urban systems rather than rivers bursting their banks.

Franzi sees water through digital simulation, using two-dimensional hydrodynamic models to track how water flows and accumulates across Berlin's sealed surfaces. Her approach to touching water is fundamentally computational—she encounters water through virtual representations that predict flooding patterns and help cities prepare for increasingly intense rainfall events.

Following devastating events like Berlin's 2016 floods and the 2021 Ahrtal catastrophe, Franzi's modeling work becomes crucial for thinking with flooding in terms of adaptation. Her approach involves creating digital representations of real landscapes, then situating these models within specific urban contexts. She touches water and flooding through bringing simulations into concrete flood management.
