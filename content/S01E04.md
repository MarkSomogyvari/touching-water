# Water Governance in Multiple Voices: A Conversation with Kei
Water governance researcher Kei Namba brings a unique cross-cultural perspective, contrasting Japan's disaster-driven risk awareness with Germany's approach to water management. Her work in the Berlin-Brandenburg region reveals water's "multiplicity"—how the same resource serves urban supply, irrigation, energy, mining, and tourism, each with different stakeholder interests.

Through qualitative content analysis of policy documents and media reports, Kei examines how diverse voices can align around common water goals. She critiques purely technocratic solutions, advocating instead for governance approaches that include local participation. Her research touches water through institutional analysis, studying actors and power dynamics rather than physical systems.

From Berlin's "sponge city" initiative to the citizen-driven Berlin Water Charter, Kei demonstrates how civil society can demand transparency and accountability in water services.

Kei Namba brought us activated charcoal as her personal water item—a simple filtering tool that embodies her belief in accessible, sustainable water practices that bridge scientific research and everyday environmental action.
