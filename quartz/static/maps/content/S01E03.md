From drought-prone Brazil to water-rich Germany, eco-hydrologist Pedro Alencar studies flash droughts through an unusual dual approach. While analyzing satellite data and cross-correlating databases to track rapid soil moisture loss, he simultaneously engages directly with local farmers and communities—a practice uncommon among natural scientists.

Pedro's research in Brandenburg reveals how flash droughts challenge traditional water-abundant regions. But rather than relying solely on modeling, he incorporates farmers' lived experiences and perceptions into his scientific framework. This community-engaged methodology enriches his data analysis while helping tailor practical solutions like water harvesting and nature-based mitigation strategies.

His approach demonstrates how touching water means understanding both its measurable patterns and its dialogue with local experts.
